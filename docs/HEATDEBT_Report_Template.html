Section 02

Why This Neighborhood Overheats

Three compounding physical factors drive this neighborhood's extreme heat. First, 89% of the surface area is impervious — dark asphalt roads, parking lots, and concrete absorb solar radiation during the day and release it as heat at night, preventing the area from cooling down.

Second, tree canopy covers only 4.1% of the area compared to 23% city-wide. Trees provide shade (blocking direct solar gain), transpire water (cooling the air), and interrupt heat absorption in pavement. Their near-total absence removes the city's most cost-effective cooling mechanism.

Third, the neighborhood lacks the green space and water features present in wealthier areas. Eastchase has parks and retention ponds; West Montgomery has neither. This is a pattern consistent with historical redlining: neighborhoods denied mortgage investment in the 1940s–60s received systematically less green infrastructure and have never recovered.
